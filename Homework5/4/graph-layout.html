A layout with zero edge crossings

Ten directed edges over six nodes, arranged so every edge can be drawn as a straight line with no crossings. The trick: A, B, F, D form a square, and C and E sit inside it as two hubs.

frame edges (A–B–F–D square) hub edges (into/out of C and E)

How to draw it

  1. Draw a square. A top-left, B top-right, F bottom-right, D bottom-left.
  2. Connect the sides in this direction only: A→B (top), A→D (left), B→F (right), F→D (bottom).
  3. Place C inside the square, upper-middle, closer to the B–F side. Draw arrows into it: A→C, B→C, F→C.
  4. Place E inside the square, lower-middle, closer to the D–F side. Draw D→E and C→E into it, and E→F out of it.
  5. That's all 10 edges, and none of them need to cross.
